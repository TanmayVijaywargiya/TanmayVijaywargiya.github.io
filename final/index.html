Projects

Predicting Immunosuppression using ML

I worked on a project to help better predict immunosuppression in populations. I used datasets from the CDC and cleaned them (implementing significance testing) to find features that were of importance: such as prior history to diabetes, chronic illness, and resident of higher air quality region. Then I trained multiple different ML models (Random Forests, Decision Trees, Linear Regression, and more) to determine how well immunosuppression an individual could be predicted.

Traffic Duration ML Models

I wanted to determine how precisely we could understand traffic duration in the Bay Area. We took a point of interest, downtown SF, and used geolocation tools to map other areas in the Bay Area to see how long it takes cars to reach downtown SF. We utilized multiple types of regressions to eventually minimize our error (implemented Lasso and Ridge) and predict properly, without overfitting, the traffic duration from any spot in the Bay to downtown SF.
